Student Assignment Explained

DC is currently reviewing its policies for how students are assigned to DC Public Schools. The advisory committee that will make policy recommendations has released the potential changes that it is considering. How would these new policies affect you and your family?

We are an independent civic group called Code for DC. We took the proposals created by the advisory committee and made this website to help explain what these policies would mean for you. As you learn about the potential changes, you'll be able to easily tell us what you think. We will collect everyone's responses, and share them with the advisory committee.

School Boundaries

Every address in DC is assigned to a school boundary, which gives students living within that boundary the right to attend a specified school or schools. These boundaries have not been comprehensively reviewed since the 1970s, even as many schools have opened or closed and the city's population has changed.

Under the new boundary proposal, your school boundary would be unchanged. This means your current neighborhood elementary school will continue to draw from the same population of students.

Feeder Patterns

Every traditional elementary school "feeds" its students to a middle and a high school. These groups of schools are known as "feeder patterns." In some cases, these patterns are currently inconsistent or result in overcrowded schools.

The advisory committee has proposed changes to the feeder patterns. Your current feeder patterns, however, would remain the same under this proposal.
